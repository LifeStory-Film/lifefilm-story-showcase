# Pelican Hill Wedding Photographer: What You Need to Know

The Resort at Pelican Hill in [Newport Beach](/blog/wedding-videography-services-orange-county) is, without question, one of the [most visually stunning wedding venues in California](/blog/best-wedding-venues-malibu-california). The coliseum pool. The ocean-view terrace. The Italian-inspired architecture against the Pacific. If you're getting married here, you already know the backdrop is exceptional.

But [exceptional venues](/blog/wedding-photographer-hummingbird-nest-ranch) require exceptional preparation. Here's what you need to know about photographing and filming a wedding at Pelican Hill.

## What Makes Pelican Hill Unique

**Architecture.** The resort is built in a Romanesque Italian style — cream-colored stone, arched colonnades, terracotta rooflines — that creates a timeless visual language. Every shot against the architecture reads as elevated and formal.

**Views.** The resort sits on the [bluffs above Crystal Cove](/blog/wedding-photographer-terranea-resort) with panoramic views of the Pacific. On a clear day, the ocean fills the background from horizon to horizon. The view creates instant context — you know where you are when you look at these photos.

**Scale.** Pelican Hill is a large property with multiple distinct zones — the Coliseum Lawn, the Villa ballrooms, the Villa Rotunda, private terrace areas. A photographer unfamiliar with the property can easily waste time navigating between locations when they should be shooting.

## Best Photography Locations at Pelican Hill

**Coliseum Pool Terrace.** This is the most famous and most photographed location at Pelican Hill. The circular pool surrounded by arched colonnades with ocean views. It looks as good as it photographs — which is rare. Early morning or late afternoon light here is spectacular.

**Ocean View Terrace.** Ceremony and cocktail hour locations with unobstructed Pacific views. Portrait backgrounds here require no setup — the ocean and sky do the work.

**Villa Rotunda.** For indoor receptions, the Villa Rotunda is a circular ballroom with a domed ceiling and chandelier that photographs beautifully from above. Request permission to access the mezzanine level for overhead reception shots.

**Gardens and pathways.** The resort is full of quiet walking paths between the villas. These offer intimate portrait locations away from guests — useful during cocktail hour when you want a few minutes with the couple.

## Lighting Challenges at Pelican Hill

The west-facing ocean views that make Pelican Hill so beautiful also create a photographic challenge. Afternoon sun hitting the Pacific creates strong backlight that can blow out backgrounds if not managed correctly. Your photographer needs to know how to expose for backlit subjects — using fill light, reflectors, or off-camera flash to balance the couple against a bright sky.

Sunset here, on the other hand, is exceptional. The [golden hour](/blog/malibu-wedding-videographer-guide) at Pelican Hill — typically 5–7pm depending on the time of year — is some of the best light you'll find anywhere in California.

## Venue Logistics for Photographers

Pelican Hill is a large, busy resort. Non-wedding guests are often present in public areas. Your photography team will need to coordinate with your venue coordinator on:

- Which areas are exclusively reserved for your event
- Load-in and vendor parking procedures
- Any photography restrictions (drones are generally not permitted)
- Timing of access to different locations during the event
