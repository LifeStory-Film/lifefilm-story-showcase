# Wedding Photography vs Videography: Do You Need Both?

This is one of the most common questions couples face when building their wedding budget. Photography has traditionally been the non-negotiable. Videography is often treated as a luxury add-on — the thing you cut when you're trying to save money.

But the calculus has shifted. Here's an honest take on both, and what actually helps couples make the right call for their specific situation.

## What Photography Does That Video Can't

Still photos are easier to live with. You can hang them on a wall, send them to your grandmother, flip through them on your phone without sitting down for eight minutes. A well-composed photograph captures something permanent in a way that's immediately readable.

Photography is also more forgiving of venue conditions. A great photographer can work in dark reception halls, mixed indoor-outdoor light, and backlit ceremonies that would challenge even experienced video teams.

And for most couples, their photo gallery is what they'll interact with most often in the years after their wedding. Instagram posts, anniversary cards, framing — it all starts with photos.

**Bottom line:** If you have to choose one, and your wedding is low-key or budget-constrained, photography is usually the right call.

## What Videography Does That Photos Can't

No photograph captures the sound of your dad crying during his toast. No photo shows your partner's face the moment you walked in. A film gives you back the motion, the voice, the music — the actual feeling of the room.

Most couples who skip video report regretting it. That's not a scare tactic — it's consistent feedback we hear from couples who come back years later wishing they'd had film.

A well-made wedding film is something you'll watch on anniversaries, show your kids, and return to in ways you can't quite predict when you're planning the wedding. It captures what photos summarize.

**Bottom line:** If you have the budget, video adds something that photography genuinely cannot replicate.

## When You Might Skip Video

There are legitimate scenarios where video makes less sense:

- You're having a very small, intimate elopement with minimal ceremony
- Your wedding is heavily budget-constrained and photo is the priority
- Your venue has strict restrictions on videography equipment or crew size

In those cases, focus your budget on the best photography you can afford.

## When You Should Definitely Get Both

- You're having a multi-ceremony wedding (South Asian, Jewish, Catholic, etc.)
- Your parents or grandparents are speaking or performing rituals that won't happen again
- Your venue is visually stunning in a way that translates well to motion
- You want something to share with people who couldn't attend

See our post on [how far in advance to book your wedding photographer](/blog/how-far-in-advance-should-you-book-your-wedding-photographer) — the same timeline applies to videographers, and booking both at the same time simplifies the process.

## The Honest Bottom Line

If budget allows: book both. You won't regret it. If you're forced to choose: photography first, with the understanding that video is the one thing most couples wish they'd added.
