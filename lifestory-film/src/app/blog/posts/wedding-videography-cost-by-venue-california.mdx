# Wedding Videography Cost by Venue in California

Wedding videography cost in California ranges from $4,500 for a single-location ranch wedding to $14,000+ for multi-day coastal resort and destination estate coverage. The price follows venue architecture more than guest count — a 100-guest wedding at Calamigos Ranch requires a 2-shooter crew for 10 hours, while the same guest count at Resort at Pelican Hill often requires 3 shooters across 12 hours to cover the clifftop ceremony, terrace cocktail hour, and ballroom reception in three distinct lighting conditions.

## How Venue Type Shapes Videography Pricing

![A romantic outdoor marriage proposal setup with heart-shaped roses and red balloons.](/blog-images/wedding-videography-cost-by-venue-california/section-1-0417cc305515.jpg)

*Photo by Prince  Nature on Pexels*


Videography packages scale to the number of distinct scenes the venue creates, not the size of the property. A single-location ranch ceremony and reception — oak grove to barn — can be covered by a 2-shooter team with minimal gear moves. A multi-level coastal resort with ceremony on the lawn, cocktails on the terrace, and dinner in a ballroom requires a 3-shooter crew, two additional hours of setup, and often a third camera for the sunset portraits between ceremony and reception.

**Ranch and vineyard venues** (Calamigos Ranch, Saddlerock Ranch, Triunfo Creek Vineyards) typically sit in one lighting zone. The ceremony happens outdoors under natural canopy, and the reception moves 200 feet into a barn or pavilion. A standard 10-hour [Wedding Film](https://lifestory.film/services/wedding-film/) package — 2 shooters, ceremony and vows coverage, toasts, and first dance — starts at $4,500. If the couple adds a [Save-the-Date Film](https://lifestory.film/services/save-the-date-film/) shot on the same property three months earlier, the combined cost runs $5,800–$6,200.

**Coastal resort properties** (Resort at Pelican Hill, Montage Laguna Beach, Terranea Resort, Bel-Air Bay Club) divide the day into three or four cinematically distinct chapters: clifftop ceremony at golden hour, sunset portraits on the lawn, cocktails on a terrace facing the ocean, and a ballroom or pavilion reception after dark. Each scene requires different lighting setups, and the distance between locations means the crew cannot reposition quickly. A 12-hour package with 3 shooters starts at $7,000. Resort weddings often pair video with [Documentary Photography](https://lifestory.film/services/documentary-photography/), and a combined [Photo + Video Combined Package](https://lifestory.film/services/photo-video-combined-package/) for a full day runs $9,500–$12,000.

**Estate and garden venues** (Hotel Bel-Air, Hummingbird Nest Ranch, Ojai Valley Inn, San Ysidro Ranch) present mixed lighting — dappled outdoor ceremonies under sycamore or eucalyptus, open-air cocktail courtyards, and indoor or tented receptions. The videographer walks the property during the site visit to map sun angles, shade pockets, and where artificial light will be needed after sunset. A 10-hour shoot with 2 shooters runs $5,500–$7,500 depending on how much of the reception happens indoors. San Ysidro Ranch weddings, which often span two days with a welcome dinner in the Stonehouse and the ceremony on the upper meadow, run $8,500–$10,000 for the full weekend.

**Destination and multi-day events** (Auberge du Soleil, Meadowood, Carmel Valley Ranch in Northern California, or international properties like Borgo Egnazia and Le Sirenuse) start at $10,000 and run to $14,000+ when travel, accommodation, and multi-day shoot fees are included. A Napa Valley wedding at Auberge du Soleil with a welcome dinner on the terrace, ceremony in the olive grove, and reception under the pavilion requires 2 full days of coverage. A [Destination Wedding Film](https://lifestory.film/services/destination-wedding-film/) package for Italy or France includes round-trip airfare for the crew, 4 nights of accommodation, and up to 16 hours of shoot time across rehearsal dinner and wedding day.

## Coverage Hours and Crew Size

![A dynamic gathering of photographers capturing moments indoors with flash.](/blog-images/wedding-videography-cost-by-venue-california/section-2-c2923f13ca98.jpg)

*Photo by Nurul Sakinah Ridwan on Pexels*


The baseline [Wedding Film](https://lifestory.film/services/wedding-film/) is 10 hours of coverage with a 2-shooter video crew. That window typically runs from final preparations (bride in her dress, groom's first look with groomsmen) through the first dance and cake cutting. For ranch and single-location venues, 10 hours is enough. For resort properties where the ceremony and reception are separated by an hour of sunset portraits and a location change, 12 hours is standard.

A **2-shooter crew** covers most weddings up to 150 guests. One videographer follows the bride, one the groom, and both converge for the ceremony. At venues like Calamigos Ranch or Triunfo Creek Vineyards, where the ceremony and reception happen within 300 feet of each other, two shooters can reposition quickly and cover toasts, first dance, and parent dances without a third.

A **3-shooter crew** is required when the venue splits the day into scenes that happen simultaneously or in rapid succession with no time to move gear. Resort at Pelican Hill cocktail hours often happen on two levels — an upper terrace bar and a lower lawn with live music — and a 2-shooter crew cannot be in both. Montage Laguna Beach weddings frequently schedule sunset portraits on the beach 200 yards from the reception terrace, and the videographer covering the portraits cannot also capture guest arrivals at cocktail hour. The third shooter costs $1,200–$1,800 for the day.

**Multi-day coverage** is common at Northern California estate venues and destination properties. A Meadowood wedding might include a welcome dinner under the oaks on Friday and the ceremony and reception on Saturday. The crew shoots 6 hours Friday and 12 hours Saturday, billed as an 18-hour two-day package. Cost runs $10,500–$12,000 depending on whether the couple wants the welcome dinner edited into the main wedding film or delivered as a separate [Highlight Reel](https://lifestory.film/services/highlight-reel/).

## What Drives Cost Variations Within a Venue Tier

![A beautifully decorated wedding cake table outdoors with lush greenery and brick walls, capturing a serene setting.](/blog-images/wedding-videography-cost-by-venue-california/section-3-5bd8b2416fe0.jpg)

*Photo by tommy picone on Pexels*


Two weddings at the same venue can differ by $2,000 in videography cost based on four variables: time of year, ceremony start time, edit deliverables, and whether the couple books [Photo + Video Combined Package](https://lifestory.film/services/photo-video-combined-package/).

**Time of year** affects travel and accommodation costs for destination venues. A winter wedding at Ojai Valley Inn costs the same in shooter fees as a summer wedding, but if the couple wants an [Engagement Film](https://lifestory.film/services/engagement-film/) shot in Ojai three months before the wedding, the winter travel window limits scheduling flexibility and can add $400 in last-minute airfare.

**Ceremony start time** changes the lighting plan. A 4 PM ceremony at Bel-Air Bay Club takes advantage of the golden-hour light filtering through the canyon and requires minimal artificial light during cocktails. A 6 PM ceremony at the same venue happens at dusk, and the cocktail hour and reception require continuous LED panels and battery packs, adding $600 in lighting rental and an extra prep hour the morning of the wedding.

**Edit deliverables** scale cost more than shoot hours. The base wedding film is an 8–12 minute narrative cut of the full day, delivered 10–12 weeks after the wedding. If the couple also wants [Full Ceremony Coverage](https://lifestory.film/services/full-ceremony-coverage/) — the complete 30-minute ceremony with processional, readings, vows, and recessional edited as a standalone piece — that adds $1,200. A 90-second [Highlight Reel](https://lifestory.film/services/highlight-reel/) optimized for Instagram adds $800. A couple booking all three deliverables (full film, full ceremony, highlight reel) from a Montage Laguna Beach wedding will spend $9,000–$10,500 depending on crew size.

**Photo and video together** always costs less than booking separately. A couple hiring LifeStory for both [Documentary Photography](https://lifestory.film/services/documentary-photography/) and a [Wedding Film](https://lifestory.film/services/wedding-film/) at Calamigos Ranch pays $8,500 for the combined 10-hour package with 2 photo shooters and 2 video shooters, versus $5,000 for video alone and $4,500 for photo alone if booked separately ($9,500 total). The discount reflects shared logistics — one site visit, one timeline coordination call with the planner, and overlapping travel.

## Comparing Costs: Ranch vs Resort vs Destination

Here is what a comparable wedding looks like across three venue tiers. The couple is the same, the guest count is 120, and the timeline is similar (4 PM ceremony, cocktails, 6:30 PM reception). Only the venue changes.

**Calamigos Ranch (ranch tier):** Oak-grove ceremony, cocktails in the courtyard, barn reception. Single lighting zone, minimal crew repositioning. 10 hours, 2-shooter video, [Wedding Film](https://lifestory.film/services/wedding-film/) deliverable. **$4,800.**

**Montage Laguna Beach (resort tier):** Lawn ceremony overlooking the Pacific, terrace cocktails, ballroom reception. Three lighting zones, 12-hour window to accommodate sunset portraits on the beach between ceremony and reception. 3-shooter crew. [Wedding Film](https://lifestory.film/services/wedding-film/) + [Highlight Reel](https://lifestory.film/services/highlight-reel/). **$9,200.**

**Auberge du Soleil (destination tier):** Welcome dinner on the terrace Friday evening, olive-grove ceremony Saturday afternoon, pavilion reception. Two-day shoot, 18 total hours, 2-shooter crew, travel and accommodation for two. [Destination Wedding Film](https://lifestory.film/services/destination-wedding-film/) combining both days into one 12-minute narrative. **$11,800.**

The cost spread reflects coverage complexity, not venue prestige. Calamigos Ranch weddings are not cheaper because the venue fee is lower; they cost less to film because the videographer sets up once and shoots in one continuous lighting condition. Montage requires three setups and a crew large enough to cover simultaneous scenes. Auberge requires two days of travel and two full shoot days.

## Package Pricing vs À La Carte

LifeStory offers both fixed packages and à la carte pricing. The **fixed packages** — Signature, Premier, and Luxury — bundle shoot hours, crew size, and deliverables at a set rate. The **à la carte model** lets the couple build exactly what they need and pay only for that.

Most couples booking coastal resort and destination weddings choose à la carte because their timeline is non-standard. A Terranea Resort wedding with a Friday welcome dinner, Saturday ceremony, and Sunday brunch farewell does not fit a templated package. The couple books 8 hours Friday, 12 hours Saturday, and 4 hours Sunday separately, and the final invoice reflects only those hours.

Ranch and vineyard weddings with a single-day timeline fit the fixed packages well. A couple booking Saddlerock Ranch for a 4 PM ceremony and evening reception books the Signature package — 10 hours, 2 shooters, one [Wedding Film](https://lifestory.film/services/wedding-film/) — and pays the flat $4,800 rate.

Fixed packages include one round of revision notes on the edit. À la carte bookings include revision notes only if explicitly added (typically $400 for one round). Most couples approve the first cut, but those who want to swap a song or extend a vows section by fifteen seconds add the revision option at booking.

## When to Book Photo and Video Together

Booking [Photo + Video Combined Package](https://lifestory.film/services/photo-video-combined-package/) saves $1,000–$1,500 over separate contracts and reduces coordination overhead. One site visit covers both teams. One timeline document governs both shooting schedules. One point of contact manages both deliveries.

The logistics advantage matters most at complex venues. A Hummingbird Nest Ranch wedding with ceremony on the hilltop, cocktails by the lake, and reception in the pavilion requires the photo and video teams to leapfrog each other — the photographer shoots bride-and-groom portraits at the lake while the videographer captures cocktail-hour toasts on the terrace, then they swap. That choreography is planned during a single site visit when both teams walk the property together. Booking separately means two site visits, two timeline calls, and the risk that the photographer and videographer propose conflicting portrait schedules the week before the wedding.

The cost for a combined package at a venue like Ojai Valley Inn or San Ysidro Ranch runs $9,500–$12,000 for 10–12 hours of coverage with 2 photo shooters and 2 video shooters. That delivers 600–800 edited photos and one [Wedding Film](https://lifestory.film/services/wedding-film/). If the couple also wants [Full Ceremony Coverage](https://lifestory.film/services/full-ceremony-coverage/) and a [Highlight Reel](https://lifestory.film/services/highlight-reel/), the package runs $11,000–$13,500.

## Travel Fees and Destination Logistics

Weddings outside Southern California incur travel fees that vary by distance and accommodation requirements. A wedding at Carmel Valley Ranch or Cavallo Point in Northern California adds $800–$1,200 in airfare and lodging for a 2-shooter crew. A wedding at Borgo Egnazia in Puglia or Le Sirenuse on the Amalfi Coast adds $3,000–$4,000 for international airfare, 4 nights of accommodation, and ground transportation.

Travel fees are billed separately from the shoot-day rate. A [Destination Wedding Film](https://lifestory.film/services/destination-wedding-film/) at Villa Cetinale in Tuscany is priced as $10,000 for 16 hours of shooting across two days, plus $3,500 for travel and lodging, totaling $13,500. If the couple wants an [Engagement Film](https://lifestory.film/services/engagement-film/) shot in Los Angeles three months before the Italy wedding, that is a separate $2,200 half-day shoot in LA with no travel fee.

Destination weddings often involve a site visit six to eight weeks before the wedding. The videographer flies to the venue, walks the property with the planner, maps the ceremony and reception spaces, and confirms power access for lighting. That visit costs $1,500–$2,000 and is optional but recommended for venues the crew has not worked at before. For repeat venues — LifeStory has shot at Auberge du Soleil, Meadowood, and San Ysidro Ranch multiple times — the site visit is often skipped because the crew already knows the property.

## Edit Turnaround and Delivery

The standard [Wedding Film](https://lifestory.film/services/wedding-film/) is delivered 10–12 weeks after the wedding date. [Highlight Reel](https://lifestory.film/services/highlight-reel/) pieces — the 90-second social cut — deliver in 6–8 weeks. [Full Ceremony Coverage](https://lifestory.film/services/full-ceremony-coverage/) delivers in 8–10 weeks because it requires less narrative shaping than the main film.

Rush delivery is available for an additional fee. A couple who wants the [Highlight Reel](https://lifestory.film/services/highlight-reel/) within 3 weeks to share on their first anniversary pays a $600 expedite fee. The full film cannot be rushed below 8 weeks because the edit process — selecting takes, color grading 400+ clips, sound mixing, and syncing vows audio — requires time to do well.

Raw footage is not included in any package and is not available for purchase. The edited film is the deliverable. Couples receive a private Vimeo link and a downloadable MP4 file optimized for archiving and sharing.

## Booking Timeline and Deposit

Most couples book videography 8–12 months before the wedding. Popular dates at venues like Resort at Pelican Hill, Montage Laguna Beach, and Ojai Valley Inn fill 12–18 months out, and those couples often book the videographer at the same time they book the venue.

The deposit is 30% of the total package cost, due at contract signing. The remaining 70% is due 30 days before the wedding. If the couple adds services after the contract is signed — upgrading from 10 hours to 12, adding a third shooter, or adding a [Highlight Reel](https://lifestory.film/services/highlight-reel/) — those additions are invoiced separately and due before the wedding date.

Cancellations follow the venue's force-majeure policy. If the venue postpones the wedding due to fire, flood, or other covered event, the deposit moves to the new date. If the couple cancels for another reason more than 90 days before the wedding, 50% of the deposit is refunded. Cancellations within 90 days forfeit the full deposit because the date cannot be rebooked.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does wedding videography cost at a California ranch venue?**  
A 10-hour wedding film package with a 2-shooter crew at a ranch venue like Calamigos Ranch or Saddlerock Ranch starts at $4,500 and runs to $6,000 depending on deliverables. Adding a highlight reel brings the total to $5,300–$6,800.

**What is the cost difference between filming at a coastal resort vs a vineyard?**  
Coastal resort weddings (Resort at Pelican Hill, Montage Laguna Beach) cost $2,000–$3,000 more than vineyard weddings due to longer shoot hours (12 vs 10), additional crew (3 shooters vs 2), and more complex lighting across multiple venue levels. Expect $7,000–$9,500 for a resort vs $4,500–$6,000 for a vineyard.

**Does a destination wedding in Northern California cost more than a Southern California wedding?**  
Yes. A Napa or Sonoma wedding at Auberge du Soleil or Meadowood adds $800–$1,200 in travel and accommodation costs on top of the shoot-day rate. A two-day destination package typically runs $10,000–$12,000 total, versus $7,000–$9,000 for a comparable single-day Southern California resort wedding.

**What does a Photo + Video Combined Package cost at a venue like San Ysidro Ranch?**  
A combined package with 10 hours of coverage, 2 photo shooters, 2 video shooters, 600+ edited images, and one wedding film runs $9,500–$11,000. Adding full ceremony coverage and a highlight reel brings the total to $11,500–$13,000.

**How much does a 3-shooter video crew add to the package cost?**  
A third videographer adds $1,200–$1,800 to the total cost. This is standard for multi-level resort venues where cocktail hour and ceremony-exit moments happen simultaneously in different locations, such as Terranea Resort or Bel-Air Bay Club.

**Are travel fees included in the wedding videography package price?**  
No. Travel fees for weddings outside Southern California are billed separately. Northern California weddings add $800–$1,200. International destination weddings in Italy, France, or Japan add $3,000–$4,000 for airfare, accommodation, and ground transport.

**When is the best time to book a wedding videographer for a California resort wedding?**  
Book 10–14 months before the wedding date. High-demand venues like Montage Laguna Beach, Resort at Pelican Hill, and Ojai Valley Inn see their peak-season weekends (May through October) fill 12–18 months in advance, and videography availability follows the same curve.

**What is the turnaround time for a wedding film after the wedding day?**  
The full wedding film delivers 10–12 weeks after the event. A highlight reel delivers in 6–8 weeks. Rush delivery (8 weeks for the full film, 3 weeks for the highlight reel) is available for a $600 expedite fee.
