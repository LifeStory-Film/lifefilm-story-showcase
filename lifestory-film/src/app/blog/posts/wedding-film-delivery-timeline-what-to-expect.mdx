# Wedding Film Delivery Timeline: What to Expect

You just got married. The adrenaline is fading, the honeymoon photos are already on your phone, and now you're wondering: when will the wedding film arrive?

This is one of the most common questions couples ask — and also one of the most misunderstood. Here's an honest breakdown of what to expect.

## Why Wedding Films Take Time

A wedding film is not a simple video export. The typical workflow for a cinematic wedding highlight film involves:

1. **Ingestion and backup** — All footage is offloaded and backed up to multiple drives. For a full-day wedding with two cameras, this can be 500GB or more of raw footage.
2. **Review and logging** — The editor watches through everything and makes selects. This step alone can take 4–6 hours.
3. **Music selection** — Finding the right licensed music that fits the tone and pacing of the edit is more time-intensive than it sounds.
4. **Rough cut** — Building the first assembly, identifying the emotional through-line, placing the key moments.
5. **Refinement** — Color grading, audio cleanup, timing adjustments, sound design.
6. **Export and QC** — Final export, quality check, upload, delivery.

For an 8–10 minute highlight film, a professional edit represents 20–40 hours of work — not counting the wedding day itself.

## Standard Delivery Timelines by Film Type

**Teaser (1–2 minutes):** 2–4 weeks. These are short, often cut to music with minimal dialogue. Faster to produce.

**Highlight film (6–12 minutes):** 8–16 weeks. This is the standard delivery window for a full cinematic highlight. 8 weeks is fast; 12–14 is common; 16 weeks is at the outer edge of reasonable.

**Full ceremony edit:** Often delivered alongside the highlight film or shortly after. A single-camera ceremony edit with clean audio can be turned around faster than the highlight. A multi-camera ceremony edit takes longer.

**Same-day edit:** Delivered the day of the wedding (during the reception). This is a separate product entirely. See our post on [same-day edit vs. highlight film](/blog/same-day-edit-vs-highlight-film-whats-the-difference) for more.

## What a Reasonable Contract Should Say

Before you sign, make sure your contract includes:

- **Specific delivery windows** — not "within a reasonable time" but actual weeks
- **What happens if they miss the window** — any remedies or communication requirements
- **Revision policy** — how many rounds of changes are included and what they cost beyond that

Vague delivery language is a red flag. A professional studio with real volume will know their average turnaround and commit to it in writing.

## What Causes Delays

- Peak wedding season (May–October in LA) means backlogs
- Outsourced editing — some studios send footage to editors overseas, which adds communication and revision loops
- Equipment failures or data loss (should be disclosed immediately)
- Music licensing issues — some videographers use music they can't legally license, which causes delivery platform rejections

## When to Follow Up

If you haven't received a communication from your videographer within 4 weeks of the wedding, a friendly check-in is reasonable. Most studios will send a "we've started your edit" note proactively. If you've hit the delivery window in your contract and heard nothing, follow up in writing and keep a record.
