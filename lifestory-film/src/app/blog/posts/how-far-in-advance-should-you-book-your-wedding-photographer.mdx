# How Far in Advance Should You Book Your Wedding Photographer?

One of the most common questions couples ask early in the planning process is: how soon do I need to book my wedding photographer? The short answer is earlier than you think. The longer answer depends on your wedding date, your venue, and how much flexibility you have on your first-choice vendor.

## The General Rule: 12 Months Out

For most weddings in Los Angeles and Southern California, booking your photographer and videographer 12 months in advance is the standard. The best photographers in your market — the ones whose work you have been saving on Instagram for months — are often booked a year or more out. If you have your heart set on a specific creative, reaching out the day after you get engaged is not too early.

This timeline gives you enough runway to:
- Review portfolios thoroughly and not make a rushed decision
- Meet with two or three candidates before committing
- Secure your first choice before they are unavailable
- Plan your photo timeline into the rest of your wedding schedule

## Peak Season Requires Earlier Action

In Southern California, peak wedding season runs roughly from **May through October**, with September and October being especially popular due to consistently good weather. June Saturdays and holiday weekends book up fastest.

If your wedding falls on a peak-season Saturday, assume that top vendors are already booking 14 to 18 months out. A September Saturday in 2027 could be claimed by mid-2026 by couples who planned ahead.

**Off-peak advantage:** If you have flexibility on date or day of the week, a Friday or Sunday wedding in November or January opens up more availability — and sometimes better pricing. Review our [pricing page](/pricing) for current package rates and availability.

## Venue Can Affect Your Timeline

Some venues have a preferred vendor list — a set of approved photographers and videographers who are familiar with the property and approved to work there. If your venue has a preferred list, check it early: if your first-choice photographer is not on it, you may need to get them approved or reconsider your options.

Venues that book out far in advance — like Pelican Hill, Greystone Mansion, or Calamigos Ranch — also attract photographers who fill their calendars quickly. Booking your venue and your creative team around the same time makes sense.

## What Happens If You Wait

If you are already inside the 12-month window, do not panic — but do move quickly. Here is what the timeline looks like in practice:

- **9–12 months out:** You still have good options, but your top choices may already be gone for peak dates. Prioritize outreach.
- **6–9 months out:** First-choice availability is hit or miss for popular dates. You may need to be flexible on date or package.
- **Under 6 months:** Harder but not impossible, especially for off-peak dates. Some photographers hold cancellation dates; it is worth asking.
- **Under 3 months:** You are likely looking at whoever is still available. Quality varies. Budget extra time to vet thoroughly.

## A Simple Booking Checklist

Once you have your venue and date confirmed, follow this sequence:

1. Identify 3–5 photographers and videographers whose work you love
2. Check their availability for your date immediately — before your first meeting
3. Schedule consultations within the first two weeks of your search
4. Ask about package inclusions, delivery timelines, and contract terms
5. Book as soon as you have found the right fit — do not wait to compare more options

The right photographer is not just the one with the best portfolio. It is the one who makes you feel comfortable, communicates clearly, and understands how you want your wedding to feel. Give yourself enough time to find that person.
