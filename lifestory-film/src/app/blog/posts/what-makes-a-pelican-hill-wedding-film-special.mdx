# What Makes a Pelican Hill Wedding Film Special

There are beautiful wedding venues, and then there is Pelican Hill. Perched on the bluffs of Newport Coast overlooking the Pacific Ocean, the Resort at Pelican Hill is in a category of its own — and the couples who choose it deserve a film that matches the grandeur of the setting.

If you are planning a wedding at Pelican Hill and searching for the right videographer, here is what makes this venue so extraordinary to film, and what to look for when choosing your [Pelican Hill wedding videographer](/pelican-hill-wedding-videographer).

## The Venue: A Filmmaker's Dream

Pelican Hill was designed with an Italian Riviera aesthetic — colonnaded walkways, hand-painted tiles, manicured gardens, and that endless view of the Pacific. Every frame of your wedding film has architectural beauty built into it before your videographer even arrives.

The resort's outdoor ceremony spaces sit at elevation, meaning the ocean is always in the background. On clear days, you can see Catalina Island on the horizon. During golden hour, the warm California light catches the white stone architecture and turns everything to gold. It is the kind of light that makes a cinematographer's job look easy — but experienced Pelican Hill videographers know it changes fast, and you have to be ready to move with it.

The colonnaded rotunda creates natural framing opportunities. The Pelican Hill ballroom has soaring ceilings and warm lighting that translates beautifully to film. Even the pathways between venues, lined with olive trees and lavender, give you natural transition moments that add texture to a wedding film.

## Why Pelican Hill Requires an Experienced Videographer

The venue's beauty is also its challenge. With so much visual richness competing for attention, a less experienced videographer can fall into the trap of shooting the venue instead of the couple. The best Pelican Hill wedding films are not travelogues — they use the setting to frame an intimate human story.

The ocean views mean dealing with bright background light during daytime ceremonies. A videographer who does not know how to expose correctly for backlit conditions will deliver footage where the couple is a silhouette against a blown-out sky. Ask any prospective videographer to show you footage shot outdoors at Pelican Hill, or at comparable ocean-view venues.

The scale of Pelican Hill also means longer distances between locations — bridal suite to ceremony site, cocktail hour to ballroom. A well-coordinated videography team knows how to transition efficiently without missing key moments.

## What Your Pelican Hill Wedding Film Should Include

A great Pelican Hill wedding film captures the full sensory experience of the day:

**The arrival and getting-ready moments.** The resort's suites are spacious and beautifully appointed. These scenes establish the tone of the film before the ceremony begins.

**The ceremony with the ocean behind you.** This is the iconic Pelican Hill shot — the one that makes the venue instantly recognizable. It should be filmed with multiple angles and a wide establishing shot that shows the full scope of the setting.

**Cocktail hour on the terrace.** The hour between ceremony and reception, with guests mingling against the backdrop of the Pacific, is some of the richest documentary footage of a wedding day.

**Reception details and the dance floor.** The ballroom at Pelican Hill is built for grand celebrations. A skilled videographer captures both the designed details — the florals, the table settings, the lighting — and the unscripted joy of the people in the room.

**Golden hour portraits.** If your timeline allows, even fifteen minutes with your videographer during sunset at Pelican Hill will produce some of the most cinematic footage of your entire film.

## Choosing the Right Pelican Hill Wedding Videographer

When evaluating videographers for your Pelican Hill wedding, look for someone who has filmed there before and can show you the work. Ask specifically about how they handle ocean-backlit ceremonies and low-light ballroom receptions.

Beyond technical skill, look for a storyteller — someone whose films make you feel the emotion of the day, not just the beauty of the setting. Pelican Hill gives your videographer an extraordinary canvas. The right filmmaker knows the story is about you, not the venue.
