For a decade we have been guests at the most considered weddings on the West Coast — quiet ceremonies in the olive groves above Auberge du Soleil, after-parties that spilled from the Polo Lounge into the gardens at Hotel Bel-Air, three-day celebrations that wound through the orchards and stone barns at Calamigos Ranch. We have stood in pantries with chefs, in suites with stylists, in the back of vintage Citroëns with grooms who refused to look at their watches. We have learned that the best of these days are not loud. They are precise.

This journal is for the readers who recognize that precision.

## What the journal is for

We started writing because clients kept asking the same kinds of questions, and none of the answers we wanted to give existed on the internet in a form we could send them. What does a film crew actually do during a tea ceremony? How does a Saturday at Sunstone Villa pace differently than a Saturday at Beaulieu Garden? When the rain comes — and in Napa in October it sometimes does — what changes about the way a story gets told?

The journal is also, frankly, a record. A way of paying attention. The wedding industry moves quickly and forgets its own taste. We would like to remember.

## What you will find here

Expect three kinds of writing, published unhurriedly. **Venue studies** — long-form pieces on the houses and estates we return to, written with the granular detail a planner would want. **Field notes** — shorter dispatches on craft: the lens we reached for during a Persian sofreh aghd, the reason we no longer film vows from the aisle, the audio rig that finally solved outdoor toasts at scale. And **conversations** with the florists, planners, and pastry chefs whose work we admire most.

You will not find listicles. You will not find SEO-stuffed roundups of "the top ten wedding trends of 2026." If a trend is worth naming, we will name it once, in context, and move on.

## A note on aesthetics

The films we make are made slowly. The journal will be, too. We would rather publish four considered essays a year than forty competent ones. If that resonates, subscribe at the bottom of the page — we send one letter per quarter, and never the kind that asks you to follow us on anything.

Welcome. We are glad you are here.
